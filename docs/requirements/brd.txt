2.0 EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
The Amafor Gladiators Football Club seeks to establish a scalable digital ecosystem to serve three primary, interconnected business functions:
1. Talent Export Platform: To position the club as a professional academy by providing international scouts with a verified, data-rich environment for player identification and structured trialist management.
2. Fan Engagement & Entertainment Hub: To build a loyal local and global fanbase through a comprehensive media destination for news, match content, and real-time competition tracking.
3. Monetization Engine: To create sustainable revenue streams through self-service advertising, fan donations, and a patronage program.
This platform is designed to maximize engagement, establish the club as a credible entity in the global football community, and generate direct revenue.

4.0 SCOPE
4.1 In-Scope
1. Development of a responsive public website (amaforgladiatorsfc.com) with a CMS for club-curated articles, match images, and videos.
2. Development of a secure portal for scouts ("Pro View").
3. Implementation of dynamic data modules for Fixtures, Goals and League Tables (updated manually via CMS).
4. Integration of third-party video hosting (YouTube/Vimeo via URL embed) and social sharing (WhatsApp).
5. Implementation of page view tracking analytics for all videos and articles (internal dashboards only).
6. Implementation of a self-service advertising platform with per-view pricing, pre-payment for view bundles, NGN and USD integration.
7. Implementation of a supporter donation program with recurring/lifetime commitment subscriptions.
8. Implementation of match lineup and goal management and display on fixture pages.
9. Implementation of supporter recognition display (name and portrait/logo) on the website homepage.
10. The platform must be secure and restrict sensitive operations to authorized staff
11. Implementation of a public Academy Trialist application system with file uploads.
12. Implementation of a secure Academy Staff portal for trialist/player lifecycle management.
13. Integration with WhatsApp Business and SMS for multi-channel communications.
14. Automated multi-channel notification system for trialist status updates.
15. The Business must ensure user data privacy and comply with NDPR requirements.
16. The system must provide reliable donation and advertising capabilities for local and international users
17. Implementation of offline-first CMS capabilities for intermittent connectivity.
18. Implementation of offline payment option for advertisers with manual verification and dual approval.
19. Implementation of enhanced minor data protection with parental consent verification.
4.2 Out-of-Scope
1. Development of native mobile applications (iOS/Android).
2. Real-time match streaming (live broadcasting).
3. E-commerce or online merchandise store.
4. Advanced data analytics or business intelligence dashboards beyond basic view counts and campaign metrics.
5. Programmatic/ad network integration (initial phase is direct sales only).
6. Advanced ad targeting beyond placement on the Home Page or pages with specific tags.
7. Ad creative design services for advertisers.
8. Advanced player performance analytics or tracking wearables integration.
9. Custom CRM system beyond defined trialist/player records.
10. WhatsApp Business API during initial 90-day approval period (interim WhatsApp Web solution will be used).
11. Fan comments on articles.
12. Site search functionality.
13. User location targeting for ads.
14. Internal messaging between academy staff.
15. Article saving functionality for fans.
16. Downloading of match videos by scouts (streaming only).

	6.4 Advertising & Monetization Platform
Reg. ID
	Priority
	Description
	BR-AD-01
	High
	The system shall allow businesses to create a registered "Advertiser" account, requiring business verification by club staff. The Commercial Manager manually reviews and approves.
	BR-AD-02
	High
	The system shall provide a self-service dashboard where verified advertisers can: (a) Upload ad creatives, (b) Select available ad positions, (c) Set total unique view count targets, and (d) Schedule campaigns.
	BR-AD-03
	High
	The system shall calculate total campaign cost based on selected position's per-view rate multiplied by advertiser's target unique view count. Process payments via Paystack for NGN and Stripe for USD.
	BR-AD-04
	Medium
	The system shall display active advertisements in designated placement zones on the public website according to campaign schedule and targeting rules.
	BR-AD-05
	Medium
	The system shall provide advertisers with a real-time campaign performance dashboard showing: (a) Total purchased views, (b) Delivered views, (c) Remaining views, (d) Contracted cost per view, (e) Effective cost per view.
	BR-AD-06
	Medium
	The system shall automatically pause ad delivery when the purchased unique view count is reached, and notify advertisers via email.
	BR-AD-07
	High
	A 'view' is counted when ≥50% of the ad creative loads in the user's browser and is visible in viewport for a continuous duration of at least 1 second, counted once per unique user per 24-hour period per ad campaign.
	BR-AD-08
	High
	Advertiser campaign dashboards shall display view delivery data with ≤5 minute latency from actual occurrence.
	BR-AD-09
	Medium
	Ad campaigns from verified advertiser accounts may be published immediately without pre-approval of creative content. The Commercial Manager reserves the right to review and remove any live ad found to violate club policy.
	BR-AD-10
	Medium
	If payment gateway is unavailable, the system shall prevent all new ad campaign purchases and display maintenance messages.
	BR-AD-11
	High
	Commercial Manager sets and can change per-view rate for each ad zone. All registered advertisers must be notified via email 30 days prior to rate changes.
	BR-AD-12
	Medium
	Campaign performance data shall be retained and accessible to advertisers for 1 year from campaign end date.
	BR-AD-13
	Medium
	Official dispute resolution process for advertisers: Complaints must be emailed to support@amaforgladiatorsfc.com. Commercial Manager must provide acknowledgement and commitment to investigate within 2 business days.
	BR-AD-14
	High
	Primary transaction currency is Naira (NGN) for local users. International transactions may be processed in USD via Stripe. The system shall display approximate Naira equivalents using CBN exchange rates.
	BR-AD-15
	Medium
	Advertisers shall have an 'Offline Payment' option where they can upload proof of bank transfer/POS receipt. Campaign activates upon manual verification by Commercial Manager with dual approval (Commercial Manager + Finance Officer).
	BR-AD-16
	Medium
	All disputes shall be governed by Nigerian law and subject to jurisdiction of Nigerian courts.
	BR-AD-17
	Low
	Mid-Article Banner insertion rule: Insert ad after first 100 words of article body. If the article <100 words, show the ad at the end of the content.

9.0 GLOSSARY
Term
	Definition
	Business Day
	Monday-Friday, excluding Nigerian federal public holidays, 9 AM - 5 PM WAT.
	View (Ad)
	When ≥50% of an ad creative is in the viewport for ≥1 continuous second, per unique user per 24-hour period per campaign.
	Unique Visitor
	A user identified by a 24-hour persistent first-party cookie.
	Player Profile
	Represents any player under contract with Amafor Gladiators FC.
	Academy Player
	A sub-category of Player Profile for individuals in the youth academy system, promoted from the trialist system.
	Trialist
	An applicant to the academy who has not yet been accepted.
	Active Advertiser
	An advertiser account that has made at least one payment in the last 90 days.
	Verified Player Profile
	A Player Profile where all mandatory fields are populated and marked as reviewed/accurate by the Data Steward.
	Pro View
	A secure portal providing access to full match archives and enhanced player data.
	Ad Zone
	A predefined placement for advertisements. Three zones: Homepage Top, Sidebar, Mid-Article.
